You've worked so, so hard.

You were the top of your class in school. People have always envied your drive and willpower. They said you were destined for great things. So why? Why do you still have this hole in your heart?

You're on a beach by your family home, where you grew up. You're spending a lot of time here lately. Thinking about that hole inside you. Trying to problem solve your way to happiness. Nothing. It's all just more weight.

It's all just more work.

Today is different. You wont take responsibility again. You can't take any more responsibility. It's all so heavy. Life is so heavy. You look up at the vast sky, and it's emptiness is reflected inside of you.

...

'Please, if there's anybody out there. Help me. I don't know how to fill this void'

'OW!'

You're jolted out of your daze by a sharp pain in your arm. it looks like sand crab crawled underneath you while you were sky-gazing. Just what you needed.

You move to flick the creature towards the ocean and get back to the problems of your heart. But... You hesitate. He scuttles towards you again, and this time you don't flinch.

Those elegant legs, the perfect, purposeful shuffle. The light crackling of his crabdomen dragging against the tiny pieces of crystalized sand.

Those eyes... In those eyes, you see the reflection of the whole universe.

You see your life, every success, every heartbreak, every day that the bottomless black pit swalowed you deeper and deeper.

You're there now, falling and falling, the world a mere blip miles above you, endlessly drowning.

A claw.

A breath of fresh air.

Time begins again.

The sun is shining. You feel it's warmth on your skin.

You feel. You feel more than you remembered you knew how to feel.

The crab scurries back towards the ocean.

Your eyes are dry. How long have you been crying?

...

..."Thank you....."

'Sir? Are you okay?

A young boy approaches you on the beach. His clothes are tattered, he smells like old milk, and he seems to think it's okay to approach strangers on important crabbing business

'B-but sir....I have no parents.'

Of course. You should have seen it earlier. Everything about this grimey boy screams 'orphan'. You drop your latest crab into your crab bucket and look the boy up and down.

'Sir, w-why are you collecting all those crabs?'

Perhaps you and this boy can help each other...

'Hey there sport...'

Your father approaches you from the road leading up to town. Can't he see you're busy? Why did he come down here?

'You've...Got some new friends, huh? That's good to see.'

'Uh, okay.. Listen, your mother and I have been really worried about you.'

'You're spending all of your time down at this beach. You never talk to us when you're at home.'

'I know that college is coming up and that the future can be scary...'

'If you need somebody to talk to, we're always here.'

'We hate to see you looking this lost and confused.'

You can't help yourself. You let out a barking laugh that echo's accross the shoreline.

'Lost, father? I've never been more found.'

'W-well okay... Just come see us if you ever want to talk.'

He lumbers back towards home. He hesitates on the way up to the street

He almost looks like he wants to look back...But no. He continues on his way.

S-sir? I just don't know what I'm supposed to do with these crabs. Do I sell them?

You turn, anger flashing on your face before you remember who you're speaking to.

No. These are your assets. They mustn't fear you. Yet.

'We absolutely cannot sell these crabs. We need as many as we can get.'

'uhh...Alright sir. But see, you haven't explained what they're all for...

You've tried. The children don't see what you see. They don't feel the crab.

Not yet anyway.

'We just need more.'

'Alright...Uh, where do we put them all?'

'My family has a garage nearby. I'll give the address. Just...Put them there.'

'Y-yes sir.'

The boy scampers off to tell his coworkers of the plan.

'Excuse me.'

An old, lanky woman stands before you, dressed in a respectable looking suit.

'My name is Headmistress Patricia Folkhorn. I run the local orphanage in town.'

You barely hear what she says. looking up at her tall figure, something strikes you.

She twitches, seeming to wait for a response. She's distant. Cold. Unstable. Her eyes...

You can see in her eyes. She's lost, just like you were.

'I think you and I should have a conversation, please follow me to my office'

She waves at the children and heads towards the street.

You find yourself in a cold office inside a leaky building. This place is so run down it's embarassing.

'I'll be honest with you. I'm confused by all this.'

'At first, I thought it was just a game. But then the children began bringing home live crabs in their bags. They seem to be taking this all quite seriously. They go on about the correct way to to address their employer, and how they are all working towards enlightenment.'

'They seem to be taking this all quite seriously. On one hand, it's great to see the children working together and Spassionate about doing something in the world. On the other hand, the law frowns upon young people who take advantage of those weakest in society for their own benefit.

I woulden't want to have to get the law involved unless I have to, so perhaps you can enlighten me as to what's really going on here.

You can't help it. Your grin slowly spreads ear to ear across your face.

You leap across the desk towards her. She falls out of her chair, and you're standing over her in an instant.

You reach into your pocket and pull out a crab, fresh from the ocean. Fresh with all the knowledge in the universe.

She doesen't yell for help. She looks...confused. She's trying to understand. This is it. You can tell. This is a beautiful moment. If she wont sing it's praises in volume, you must fill the gap.

'SEE WHAT I SEE. UNDERSDTAND AS I HAVE UNDERSTOOD. OPEN YOURSELF TO THE CRAB, AS I HAVE DONE.'

Sweat is beading down her face. She gazes at it, a mixture of curiosity, bewilderment, and horror displayed on her features.

Tears are rolling down her face. She see's it. Just as you've seen it.

You return the crab to it's rightful place in your pocket. Always accessible - Good crab - and turn to leave.

She needn't say anything. She understands now. That is enough. You stop in the doorway.

'You will not interrupt my work again.'

She seems to wake from her tance. Is that all the time that passed for you, too? It felt like all of time had elapsed.

'Wait! Please! I can help you! I must help you!"
